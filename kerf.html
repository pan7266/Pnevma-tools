Focus depth

Focus depth is measured from the top surface. Positive values place the waist inside the material; negative values place it above the surface.

Optical taper tendency

Optical taper tendency compares top, middle, and bottom beam diameters. It is not a real-world taper angle.

Confidence logic

Confidence increases with matching optical profiles, known material subtype, preset thickness, and measured kerf. It drops for coated, unknown, or uncalibrated materials.

Beam through material

Beam diameter is an optical indicator, not the kerf. Real kerf depends on material, heat, plume, air assist, extraction, optics, alignment, and beam quality.

Expected kerf behavior

Beam diameter is an optical indicator only. Actual kerf depends on material absorption, melting, plume shielding, air assist, extraction, resin, density, backing films, lens cleanliness, alignment, and beam quality.
